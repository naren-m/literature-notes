Migration of the **Amara Kosham** hub page from Notion (ID `c51dd62d13da41aeacb986812a244f5c`). The hub organised a personal study plan around the **Amarakosha** (अमरकोशः) — a classical Sanskrit thesaurus by Amarasimha (~6th c. ## About the Amarakosha

The Amarakosha ("Immortal Dictionary", also called *Nāmaliṅgānuśāsana*) is the oldest and most influential Sanskrit thesaurus (*kośa*). It is organised into three *kāṇḍas* (chapters), each subdivided into *vargas* (categories); each varga groups the synonyms for a semantic field (gods, time, rivers, plants, social classes, emotions, etc.). Entries are in verse (anuṣṭubh metre) so they could be memorised.

Because the Amarakosha encodes gender (*liṅga*) alongside meaning, traditional Sanskrit students memorised it wholesale — it is still recited in modern pāṭhaśālās. The chapters in order:

1. **[[amarakosha-chapter-1]]** — *Swarga-kāṇḍa* — celestial, abstract, speech, music, time
2. **[[amarakosha-chapter-2]]** — *Bhū-kāṇḍa* — earthly things, humans, flora, fauna, society
3. **[[amarakosha-chapter-3]]** — *Sāmānya-kāṇḍa* — miscellany, grammar, homonyms, indeclinables

## How this hub was used

From the Notion structure, the author was maintaining this as a **self-learning Amarakosha chanting project**: the three chapter pages had full verse text (encoded with Avinash Sathaye / Arun's long-standing ITRANS-style transliteration available on sanskritdocuments.org); the reference PDFs and CSVs were companion study aids; the YouTube lesson was the entry point for the chanting practice.

## Canonical external source

The definitive Devanagari text of the Amarakosha is at **sanskritdocuments.org** (the "amarakosha sanskrit documents" bookmark). That source of truth should be preferred for any rigorous scholarly reference — Notion held a copy but the canonical encoding lives at sanskritdocuments.org, maintained by the community.
