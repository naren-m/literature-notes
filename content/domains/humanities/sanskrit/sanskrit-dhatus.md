## About Dhātus

A **dhātu** (धातु, literally "constituent, element") in Sanskrit grammar is a **verbal root**: an abstract semantic unit with fixed meaning from which all concrete verb forms and a very large fraction of noun forms are derived. The classical count is **~2,000 dhātus** (the *Dhātu-pāṭha* attached to Pāṇini's Aṣṭādhyāyī lists 1,940 or thereabouts, with some variants).

### Ten gaṇas (conjugation classes)

Pāṇini groups dhātus into ten conjugation classes (*gaṇas*) based on how they form their present-tense stem. Each class is named after its first root:

1. **Bhvādi** (भ्वादि) — root *bhū* "to be"; ~1,000 roots, takes -*a*- augment (e.g. *bhavati* "he/she becomes")
2. **Adādi** (अदादि) — root *ad* "to eat"; athematic, no augment (*atti* "he eats")
3. **Juhotyādi** (जुहोत्यादि) — root *hu* "to sacrifice"; reduplicating (*juhoti*)
4. **Divādi** (दिवादि) — root *div* "to shine/play"; takes -*ya*-
5. **Svādi** (स्वादि) — root *su* "to press"; takes -*nu*-
6. **Tudādi** (तुदादि) — root *tud* "to strike"; takes -*a*- with accent on suffix
7. **Rudhādi** (रुधादि) — root *rudh* "to obstruct"; nasal infix
8. **Tanādi** (तनादि) — root *tan* "to stretch"; takes -*u*- or -*o*-
9. **Kryādi** (क्र्यादि) — root *krī* "to buy"; takes -*nā*- / -*nī*-
10. **Curādi** (चुरादि) — root *cur* "to steal"; takes -*aya*- (causative-like)

### Why dhātus matter

- **Etymological transparency.** Sanskrit vocabulary is, to a remarkable extent, compositional from dhātu + suffix. The word *karma* (action) comes from *√kṛ* "to do" + suffix *-man*; *jñāna* (knowledge) from *√jñā* "to know" + *-ana*. Given the dhātu-pāṭha, a reader can decompose most Sanskrit words.
- **Derivational productivity.** Pāṇini's grammar generates nouns, adjectives, participles, causatives, desideratives, frequentatives, and compound forms from the same root — meaning a single dhātu anchors dozens of derived words.
- **Comparison to IE cognates.** Sanskrit dhātus are often directly cognate with Proto-Indo-European roots: *√bhū* ~ PIE *\*bheuh₂- ~ English "be"; *√vid* "to know" ~ PIE *\*weid- ~ Greek *oida*, Latin *video*, English "wit". Historical linguistics leans heavily on the dhātu-pāṭha.

### Yāska's standpoint

In the [[niruktam]], Yāska quotes **Śākaṭāyana**'s position that *all* nouns derive from verbal roots (the radical "dhātu-only" theory). The Notion page implicitly adopts this stance when it says "the only dictionary ever required in Sanskrit is this list of Dhatus and their meanings" — this is essentially Śākaṭāyana's view, still debated in modern morphology.
