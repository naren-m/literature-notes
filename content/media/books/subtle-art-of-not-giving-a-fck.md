## Thesis (standard framing, from memory of the book)

Manson's argument is deliberately provocative but philosophically Stoic-flavoured:

- Human energy is finite; **you only have so many fucks to give**.
- The good life is not about caring about *more* things but about caring about **fewer, better** things.
- Happiness is solving problems, not the absence of them. "Don't hope for a life without problems. Hope for a life of good problems."
- You are constantly choosing what to suffer for — the question is whether you chose well.
- Orwellian honesty about your own nose: **see yourself clearly, even (especially) when it's uncomfortable**.
