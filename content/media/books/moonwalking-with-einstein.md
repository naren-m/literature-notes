# Moonwalking with Einstein

Joshua Foer's journey of becoming a memory champion, with side-trips into two famous neurological cases — *S*, who remembered everything, and *EP*, who remembered nothing.

## Remembering names

- Pay attention — most forgetting is actually never-encoding.
- Tie the name to a vivid, funny, embodied image in the moment you hear it.

## Memory Palace (method of loci)

Pick a place you know intimately and place the items you want to remember in specific, well-known locations along a mental walk through it.

- Invented in ancient Rome to memorise speeches.
- *Topic* derives from Greek *topos* (place).
- "In the first place" — literally, the first location of your Memory Palace.
- For speeches, don't memorise words — memorise *topics* and let the words emerge.

## The Major System

Encode digits as consonants, then build pronounceable words around them:

```
0 → s/z       5 → L
1 → t/d       6 → sh/ch/j
2 → n         7 → k/g (hard)
3 → m         8 → f/v
4 → r         9 → p/b
```

Example: 52 → L + N → **Lion**. Longer numbers become phrases or scenes.
