# Zero to One — Peter Thiel

Peter Thiel & Blake Masters's *Zero to One: Notes on Startups, or How to Build the Future* (2014). "Want to read" status — no personal notes yet.

## Thesis (standard framing)

The book's central idea is the distinction between:

- **1 → n** (horizontal progress): copying things that work. Globalisation. Incremental.
- **0 → 1** (vertical progress): inventing something new. Technology. Discontinuous.

Thiel's argument: the next Bill Gates will not build an operating system; the next Larry Page will not build a search engine. The valuable companies of the future solve problems no one else is solving.

## Key concepts to expect

- **The contrarian question**: "What important truth do very few people agree with you on?"
- **Monopoly vs. competition**: competition is for losers; durable businesses are (temporary) monopolies with switching costs, network effects, economies of scale, and/or brand.
- **The last-mover advantage**: be the last great development in a specific market and enjoy years of monopoly profits.
- **The seven questions every business must answer**: engineering, timing, monopoly, people, distribution, durability, secret.
- **Definite vs. indefinite optimism/pessimism** — four worldviews; Thiel argues the West slid from definite optimism (1950s America) to indefinite optimism (post-1980s) to its detriment.
- **Secrets** — there are still important truths left to discover; the anti-secret consensus is why "great stagnation" feels real.
