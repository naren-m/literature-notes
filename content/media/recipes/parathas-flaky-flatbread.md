# Parathas — Flaky Flatbread

A great textural experience and easy to make at home. Freezes well.

**Uses:** with curries, dals, vegetable dishes; Kathi roll wrapper; sweet (cinnamon + sugar); leftover wrap.

## Ingredients

- 500 g all-purpose flour (or 50% mix with whole wheat)
- 60 g softened ghee or butter (for spreading)
- Salt, oil, water (to dough)

## Method

1. Mix flour, salt, and oil in a bowl until oil coats the flour. Add water; mix by hand until no dry flour remains. Cover; rest 15 minutes.
2. After rest, knead about 5 minutes until the dough passes the gluten window test. Divide into 8 (large) or 10 (small) pieces. Cover; rest another 15 minutes.
3. Flour the surface and pin. Roll each piece into a thin disc (slightly translucent). Spread softened ghee all over; sprinkle a little flour on top.
4. Fold in roughly 1-inch increments to make a layered rectangle. Roll the rectangle into a tight coil; seal the end. Press flat. Repeat for all pieces. Rest 15 minutes.
5. Roll each coiled disc into a circle ~8 inches across. Cook one immediately and repeat for each.
6. To cook fresh: medium heat, ghee in pan. Cook each side until golden brown — dark spots OK. Wrap in foil to hold while cooking the rest.
7. **First-cook freezing method:** medium-low pan, cook 2–3 min per side until no dry spots. Cool on a wire rack. Stack with parchment between, freeze.
8. From frozen: medium heat with ghee/oil. Cook until crispy and golden, flip, cook until cooked through. Eat immediately.
