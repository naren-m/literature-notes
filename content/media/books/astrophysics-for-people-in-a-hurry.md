# Astrophysics for People in a Hurry

A short tour of modern physics by Neil deGrasse Tyson, pitched at the rush-hour reader. Five Readwise-synced highlights captured.

## Key claims

- **Einstein's general relativity (1916)** — matter and energy curve the fabric of space and time surrounding them. This is the modern understanding of gravity.
- **Max Planck** — German physicist, introduced quantized energy in 1900, generally credited as the father of quantum mechanics. Planck units are named after him.
- **Bosons** — named for the Indian scientist **Satyendra Nath Bose**.
- **Leptons** — from Greek *leptos*, meaning "light" or "small."
- **Quarks** — name has a literary (not etymological) origin. Quirky beasts: unlike protons (+1) and electrons (–1), quarks carry *fractional* charges, which come in thirds.
