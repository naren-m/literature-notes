## Amarakosha (Amara Kosha — कोशः)

The **Amarakosha** is a 6th-century Sanskrit thesaurus by Amarasimha, organising the vocabulary of Sanskrit into three chapters (kāṇḍas) that themselves contain classified vargas (categories). In the Notion archive, the Amara Kosham hub and its three chapter pages held the full Devanagari text of all three chapters in verse form (encoded by Avinash Sathaye and Arun, as noted in the original pages).

| Chapter | Focus | Sections (vargas) | Local note |

| 2 — Bhū-kāṇḍa | Earthly things (humans, society, flora, fauna, geography) | Bhū, Pura, Śaila, Vanauṣadhi, Simha, Manuṣya, Brāhma, Kṣatriya, Vaiśya, Śūdra | [[amarakosha-chapter-2]] |
| 3 — Sāmānya-kāṇḍa | Miscellany, grammar, synonyms, homonyms | Viśeṣya-nighna, Saṃkīrṇa, Nānārtha, Avyaya, Liṅgādi-saṅgraha | [[amarakosha-chapter-3]] |

## Linguistics and grammar

- **[[sanskrit-dhatus]]** — "The Complete List of Dhatus (Sanskrit Root Words) and their English meaning" — HitXP external article. The Dhatus are the ~2,000 root verbs from which all Sanskrit words are derived via grammar rules; the page is a bookmark plus introductory framing on why a Dhatu dictionary is "the only dictionary ever required in Sanskrit".
- **[[niruktam]]** — Bookmark to Yāska's **Nirukta** (the oldest Sanskrit etymology treatise, ~6th c. BCE) via vedicheritage.gov.in flipbook reader. Pair with [[upanishads]] and [[ashtadhyayi]] in the existing sanskrit-literature directory for the Vedāṅga context.
- **[[maheshvara-sutras]]** — The 14 short sutras (Shiva Sutras) whose letter-order defines the Sanskrit alphabet's pratyāhāra system and underpins Panini's Ashtadhyayi. Full article content (Matheson Trust audio recitation context + 47 phoneme enumeration + Varna-mala / Garland of Letters).
- **[[sanskrit-chandas-meters]]** — `github.com/sanskrit/chandas` — code to recognise Sanskrit metres (sama-vṛtta, ardha-sama, viṣama, jāti/āryā) by classifying laghu/guru syllable patterns. Full README migrated for reference.
- **[[katapayadi-scheme]]** — The kaṭapayādi consonant-to-digit encoding scheme applied to the 72 Carnatic mēḷakarta rāgas, as venkaTamakhi (18th c.) used it to make the rāga number recoverable from its name. Full explanation from rksanka.tripod.com migrated verbatim.
- **[[planetary-linguistics]]** — Cross-language names of the planets (Latin, Greek, Sanskrit, Mandarin, Japanese, Thai, etc.) from nineplanets.org, including etymology of day-of-the-week names from planetary astrological origins. Resources DB row — mirrored here because of the Sanskrit linguistic thread.

## Ayurveda / Sanskrit medical verses

- **[[ayurveda-health-verses]]** — "Health Verses in Sanskrit: 10 Habits for great health according to Ayurveda" from resanskrit.com. Full text migrated: includes Charaka Saṃhita definitions of Ayu, Dharma-Artha-Kāma-Mokṣa framework, Samānya/Viśeṣa, Pañca-Mahābhūta, Tridoṣa (Vāta / Pitta / Kapha), Brāhmī Muhūrta, Abhyaṅga, Udvartana, oil pulling, adhyaśana, dinner-skipping fasting, and the closing **भोजनान्ते पिबेत्तक्रं / वासरान्ते पिबेत्पयः / निशान्ते च पिबेद्वारि — त्रिभिर्रोगो न जायते** verse.

## Readwise book highlights (Sanskrit-adjacent)

- **[[india-max-muller-highlights]]** — 14 Kindle highlights from F. Max Müller's *India* (public-domain text, Amazon ASIN B09R4TBJ5S).
