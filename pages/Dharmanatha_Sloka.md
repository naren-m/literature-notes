# Dharmanātha Śloka — Six Qualities of an Ideal Husband

## The Śloka

> **śloka॥ kāryeṣu yogī, karaṇeṣu dakṣaḥ**
> **rūpe ca kṛṣṇaḥ, kṣamayā etu rāmaḥ**
> **bhogyeṣu tṛptaḥ, sukhaduḥkhe mitraṃ**
> **ṣaṭkarmayuktaḥ khalu dharmanāthaḥ**

### Translation
*"He who is endowed with these six qualities is indeed the dharmanātha — the upholder of dharma [the ideal husband]."*

### Source
Traditional Nītiśāstra / Smṛti verse. Exact textual attribution under research.

---

## Six Qualities — Etymology & Root Analysis

### 1. కార్యేషు యోగీ — kāryeṣu yogī
*"A yogi in his duties"*

| Component | Root | Dhātupāṭha | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| **kārya** | √kṛ | 08.0010 — *karaṇe* | that which is to be done; duty |
| **yogī** | √yuj | 07.0007 — *yoge* | one absorbed in union/focus |

- **kārya** (कार्य) = effect, duty — from **√kṛ** (to do, to make); the gerundive form, "that which must be done"
- **yogī** (योगी) = one who has achieved yoga — from **√yuj** (to yoke, to unite, to focus)
- In work, he brings the total absorption of a yogī — present, undistracted, completely yoked to the task

---

### 2. కరణేషు దక్షః — karaṇeṣu dakṣaḥ
*"Skilled in execution and means"*

| Component | Root | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **karaṇa** | √kṛ | instrument, means, the "how" |
| **dakṣa** | √dakṣ | capable, adept, dexterous |

- **karaṇa** (करण) = instrument, means of action — same root √kṛ as kārya, but here emphasizing the *instrument* (karaṇa vibhakti)
- **dakṣa** (दक्ष) = adept, capable — related to Dakṣa Prajāpati, the supremely competent creator; from **√dakṣ** (*vṛddhau* — to grow, to be capable)
- Distinction from quality 1: *kārya* = knowing *what* to do; *karaṇa* = knowing *how* to do it

---

### 3. రూపేచ కృష్ణః — rūpe ca kṛṣṇaḥ
*"Like Kṛṣṇa in form and beauty"*

| Component | Root | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **rūpa** | √rūp | form, beauty, appearance |
| **kṛṣṇa** | √kṛṣ | he who attracts all |

- **rūpa** (रूप) = form, beauty — from **√rūp** (*rūpayati* — to give form, to manifest)
- **kṛṣṇa** (कृष्ण) = from **√kṛṣ** (to draw, to attract, to plough) — literally *"he who attracts all beings to himself"*; also carries the meaning of the color dark/blue — consciousness that absorbs all light
- This quality is not merely physical — Kṛṣṇa's beauty is the outward expression of inner completeness

---

### 4. క్షమయా ఏతు రామః — kṣamayā etu rāmaḥ
*"Let him approach Rāma's standard in forbearance"*

| Component | Root | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **kṣamā** | √kṣam | patience, forbearance, forgiveness |
| **etu** | √i | let him approach, may he equal |
| **rāma** | √ram | he in whom all delight |

- **kṣamā** (क्षमा) = forbearance — from **√kṣam** (*sahane* — to endure, to tolerate, to forgive)
- **etu** = 3rd person singular imperative of **√i** (to go) — *"may he come up to Rāma's level"*
- **rāma** (राम) = from **√ram** (*krīḍāyām* — to delight, to play, to be at rest) — *"he in whom all find delight"*
- Notably the śloka uses the optative *etu* only here — suggesting kṣamā is the most aspirational quality

---

### 5. భోజ్యేషు తృప్తః — bhogyeṣu tṛptaḥ
*"Content in enjoyments"*

| Component | Root | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **bhogya** | √bhuj | that which is to be enjoyed/eaten |
| **tṛpta** | √tṛp | satisfied, content, fulfilled |

- **bhogya** (भोग्य) = enjoyment, food — from **√bhuj** (*bhoge* — to enjoy, to eat, to experience); gerundive = "that which is for enjoyment"
- **tṛpta** (तृप्त) = satisfied — from **√tṛp** (*tṛptau* — to be satiated); past passive participle — *"one who has been made full"*
- The quietly most practical quality: he receives what is offered with genuine contentment

---

### 6. సుఖదుఃఖే మిత్రం — sukhaduḥkhe mitram
*"A friend in joy and sorrow alike"*

| Component | Root | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **sukha** | su + kha | good axle-hole → ease, joy |
| **duḥkha** | dus + kha | bad axle-hole → suffering, difficulty |
| **mitra** | √mid | friend, companion, one who binds |

- **sukha** (सुख) = *su* (good) + *kha* (axle-hole) — a wheel running smoothly; life well-aligned
- **duḥkha** (दुःख) = *duḥ* (bad) + *kha* — a wheel with a bad axle; misalignment, friction
- **mitra** (मित्र) = friend — from **√mid** (to bind affectionately); also a Vedic solar deity, the binder of agreements
- Crucially uses **mitra** (friend) not **pati** (lord) — a deliberately egalitarian image
- **sukhaduḥkhe** is a dvandva compound in the locative dual — both held as one inseparable reality

## Architectural Observation

The śloka pairs **cosmic ideals** with **domestic virtues**:

| Quality | Archetype | Domain |
|---|---|---|
| yogī | Cosmic absorption | Work |
| dakṣa | Practical competence | Execution |
| kṛṣṇa | Divine beauty | Appearance |
| rāma + kṣamā | Dharmic forbearance | Character |
| tṛpta | Contentment | Daily life |
| mitra | Friendship | Relationship |

The final word **dharmanāthaḥ** = *dharma* + *nātha* (lord, protector — from **√nāth**, to seek refuge, to protect). The traditional word for husband is redefined: not as possessor of wealth or status, but as one who embodies these six inner qualities.
