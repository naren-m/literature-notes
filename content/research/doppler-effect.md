# Doppler Effect

Research stub on the Doppler effect — the shift in observed wave frequency when source and observer are in relative motion. The Notion page was empty; this is the note it could have been.

## The phenomenon

When a wave source moves **toward** an observer, successive wave crests are emitted from progressively closer positions. The crests bunch up — the observed wavelength shortens and frequency increases (**blueshift** for light, higher pitch for sound).

When the source moves **away**, crests are emitted from progressively farther positions. The observed wavelength stretches and frequency decreases (**redshift** for light, lower pitch for sound).

Classic example: the siren of a passing ambulance. Pitch is higher as it approaches, drops as it passes and recedes.

## The formulas

### Classical Doppler (for sound, or light in a non-relativistic limit)

For a source moving with velocity `vₛ` and an observer with velocity `v₀` along the line connecting them, through a medium where the wave speed is `v`:

```
f' = f · (v + v₀) / (v − vₛ)
```

Sign convention: velocities positive when moving toward each other.

### Relativistic Doppler (light)

For light in vacuum, there's no medium — the formula depends only on the relative velocity `u` and Lorentz factor `γ = 1/√(1 − u²/c²)`:

```
f' = f · √((1 − β) / (1 + β))     where β = u/c, positive if receding
```

## Applications

- **Radar guns** — measure the Doppler shift of a microwave signal reflected off a moving vehicle.
- **Medical ultrasound** — Doppler mode measures blood flow velocity inside arteries.
- **Astronomy — stellar spectroscopy.** Spectral lines of distant galaxies are systematically **redshifted**, Hubble's observation that established the expanding universe.
- **Cosmic redshift** — the expansion of space itself stretches wavelengths of photons in flight. (Strictly this is cosmological rather than kinematic Doppler, but it's often lumped in.)
- **Exoplanet detection — radial velocity method.** A star wobbles slightly due to orbiting planets; the Doppler shift in its spectrum reveals unseen companions.
- **Doppler radar weather** — frequency shift of radar returns from precipitation reveals wind speeds.
