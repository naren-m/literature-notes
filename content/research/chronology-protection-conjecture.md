# Chronology Protection Conjecture

Stephen Hawking's **chronology protection conjecture** (1992) proposes that the laws of physics conspire to prevent time travel at macroscopic scales — even though general relativity, in isolation, appears to permit closed timelike curves (CTCs) in certain exotic spacetimes.

## The problem

General relativity allows solutions that contain **closed timelike curves** — paths through spacetime that loop back to their past. Notable examples:

- **Gödel universe** (1949) — a rotating cosmological solution where every point lies on a CTC.
- **Tipler cylinder** — an infinite rotating cylinder.
- **Wormholes** threaded with exotic (negative-energy) matter, arranged to let one mouth age slower than the other.
- **Kerr black holes** — inside the inner horizon, CTCs appear.

If any of these were physically realizable, grandfather-paradox questions would be more than philosophy.

## Hawking's claim

> "The laws of physics do not allow the appearance of closed timelike curves."

Mechanisms proposed:

1. **Quantum vacuum divergence.** As a spacetime evolves toward forming a CTC, quantum field fluctuations pile up along the almost-closed null geodesics. The vacuum stress-energy diverges, and the back-reaction on the geometry prevents the CTC from actually forming.
2. **Exotic matter unavailability.** Wormhole-based time machines require matter violating the null energy condition in bulk, and averaged energy-condition theorems suggest this isn't achievable in practice.
3. **No-go theorems** based on topology change and global hyperbolicity.

## Status

- Still a **conjecture** — not a theorem. Hawking called it "conjecture" specifically because no general proof exists.
- Most working physicists treat it as probably true but acknowledge we lack the quantum gravity needed to settle it.
- Stephen Hawking's famous "Chronology Protection Agency" joke: whatever enforces this, it keeps the universe safe for historians.

## Key terms

- **Closed timelike curve (CTC)** — a worldline that returns to its own past.
- **Chronology violation** — the existence of CTCs in a spacetime.
- **Chronological censorship** — a cousin principle analogous to cosmic censorship (which hides singularities behind event horizons).

## Related reading

- Morris, Thorne & Yurtsever (1988) — wormhole-based time machines.
- Hawking (1992) — original chronology protection paper.
- Kip Thorne, *Black Holes and Time Warps* — accessible book-length treatment.
