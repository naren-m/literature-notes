# Health Verses in Sanskrit — Ten Daily Habits per Ayurveda

Migrated in full because the verse-plus-commentary format is itself a substantive study resource.

## Foundational concepts

### Ayurveda defined

Ayurveda is the *upaveda* (sub-Veda) of the Atharvaveda; also called the *Pañcamaveda* (5th Veda). The word breaks into *āyu* + *veda*:

> **शरीरेन्द्रिय सत्वात्मा संयोगो।** — चरक संहिता १।४२
> *śarīrendriya satvātmā saṃyogo*
> "The union of Śarīra (body), Indriya (sense organs), Satva (mind) and Ātma (soul)."

So Ayurveda = knowledge of the union of body, senses, mind, and soul. When people bless with *"dīrghāyuṣī bhava"* (दीर्घायुषी भव) — "may you have a long life" — they mean: may you remain in union with all these factors for a long time.

### The goal

> **स्वस्थस्य स्वास्थ्य रक्षणं, आतुरस्य विकार प्रशमनं।** — चरक संहिता सूत्र ३०।२६
> *svasthasya svāsthya rakṣaṇaṃ, āturasya vikāra praśamanaṃ*
> "To maintain the health of a healthy person, and to manage or cure the diseases (mind, body, or both) that manifest in a person."

Keeping a healthy person healthy is *higher priority* than curing disease — prevention first, treatment second.

### Sāmānya and Viśeṣa

> **समान्यं एकत्वकरं, विशेषस्तु प्रुथक्त्व क्रुत्।**
> **सर्वदा सर्व भावानां सामान्यं व्रुद्धि कारणं, ह्रास हेतुर्विशेषस्च।** — चरक संहिता सूत्र १।४५

*Sāmānya* = the principle of a dravya (substance) that brings union or increase in its quality/quantity.
*Viśeṣa* = the principle that brings non-union or decrease.

Example: when skin is dry, apply moisturiser. The moisture content increases by a *similar* moisture from the product (sāmānya); the dryness is reduced by the *vishesha* (opposite) of moisture. For a drug to act, both principles come into play.

### Pañca-Mahābhūta (5 proto-elements)

Every substance (*dravya*) is made of the five proto-elements in different proportions:

1. **Pṛthvī** (earth) — hardness, bones
2. **Āp** (water) — fluidity, unctuousness
3. **Agni** (fire) — temperature, digestion, metabolism
4. **Vāyu** (air) — movement, enzyme transport, bodily motion
5. **Ākāśa** (space) — hollowness, lungs, heart chambers

### The three Doṣas

The 5 Mahābhūtas combine into 3 **doṣas** that maintain physiology when stable and cause disease when unstable:

- **वात (Vāta)** — movement. Every atom's motion; respiration, peristalsis, excretion, nerve impulses, initiation of action.
- **पित्त (Pitta)** — transformation. Digestion, metabolism, temperature changes, enzymatic breakdown.
- **कफ (Kapha)** — structure and binding. Energy storage, new-tissue formation, immunity, synovial fluid, mucous linings, adipose tissue, mental stability.

## The 10 habits

### Habit 1 — Wake in Brāhmī Muhūrta

Wake **~96 minutes before sunrise**. *Brahma* = knowledge; this is the time to seek knowledge (*Brāhmī Muhūrta*).

**Modern basis:** Body temperature is minimum at 5:00 AM. Melatonin (sleep-inducing) is at its minimum in the day; Cortisol (which helps withstand stress) is secreted more at this time. Nascent oxygen in the environment is at 40% — the more awake you are then, the more oxygen reaches your tissues. Waking in Brāhmī Muhūrta syncs your body with the environment and your internal diurnal cycles.

### Habit 2 — Vyāyāma (daily exercise)

> **शरीरायास जनकं कर्म व्यायाम उच्यते** — commentary by Arunadatta on Aṣṭāṅga Saṃgraha Sūtra 2।14
> "Any action that makes one tired is termed *vyāyāma*."

> **अर्धशक्त्या निषेव्यस्तु बलिभिः स्निग्धभोजिभिः।**
> **शीतकाले वसन्ते च मन्दमेव ततो अन्यदा।।** — Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya Sūtra 2।11
>
> One who is strong and eats unctuous food every day should use half of their energy doing vyāyāma (in winter and autumn). In summer and rainy season, less than half.

**Basis:** Vyāyāma reduces Kapha Doṣa and Medha Dhātu, main causes of *prameha* (diabetes and related), *raktacāpa* (atherosclerosis, hypertension), etc.

**Exceptions:** Not recommended for elderly (>70), young (<16), or those with Vāta/Pitta diseases — because for the elderly, tissues (dhātus) are already depleting and exercise would further deplete them; for the very young, dhātus aren't fully matured.

**Research:** Physically active people show 30–40% relative-risk reduction for colon cancer; women show 20–30% relative-risk reduction for breast cancer (NCBI PMC1402378).

### Habit 3 — Abhyaṅga (daily oil application)

**Abhyaṅga** = applying oil in *anuloma gati* (direction of body hair) all over the body. Benefits: reduces premature aging, reduces tiredness, pacifies Vāta Doṣa, brings eye clarity, nourishes body, facilitates sleep, makes skin healthy.

If full-body isn't possible, apply at minimum to **face, scalp, ears, feet**.

- **Oil:** sesame or coconut.
- **Timing:** before bath, after exercise, in the morning.
- **After:** warm-water bath.
- **Caution:** remove ear oil with a cotton wick (count to 100 of the number 1); don't press hard; spread in swift motions in the direction of hair for ≥15 minutes.

**Research:** A pilot study found Abhyaṅga reduces subjective stress, BP, and heart rate in pre-hypertensive subjects. A study on basketball players found Abhyaṅga significantly increased muscle strength; 15 days of no Abhyaṅga reduced it.

### Habit 4 — Udvartana (herbal powder massage)

Powder (typically turmeric + triphala + gram flour in equal quantity) applied after abhyaṅga and before bath.

**Research:** Significantly reduces body weight, BMI, waist circumference, skin-fold thickness; enhances skin texture; reduces stress (IAMJ article).

### Habit 5 — Oil pulling (gaṇḍūṣa)

- **Oil:** sesame or coconut.
- **Procedure:** Take a spoon of warm oil in mouth and move it until there is excessive salivation, thin watery discharges from eyes and nose.
- **Research:** Significantly reduces plaque and gingival index; reduces microbial colony count in mouth (NCBI PMC5514430).

### Habit 6 — Avoid adhyaśana (eating before the previous meal is digested)

> **अध्यशनं भूयो भुक्तस्योपरि भोजनं।** — Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya Sūtra 8।34
> "Eating food even before the digestion of the previous meal is called *adhyaśana*."

**Basis:** When you overeat, the stomach expands, puts pressure on other organs, produces more HCl (heartburn), and your body tries to metabolise the extra calories → feeling temporarily hot or dizzy.

### Habit 7 — Mindful eating

Positive vs. negative thoughts during eating, and how thankful you are for the food, impact psychology and digestion. The **cephalic phase** of digestion (beginning when we see, smell, think about food) works only when relaxed and attentive; stress shuts down digestion.

**Research:** Mindful eating as complementary treatment for obesity/overweight was found more effective than medicines alone (NCBI PMC5948727).

### Habit 8 — Ginger + rock salt before meals

Chewing a small slice of ginger with a pinch of rock salt before meals stimulates digestion.

**Research:** Ginger is antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, anti-nausea, anticancer (NCBI NBK92775). Rock salt is an antioxidant, maintains BP, reduces cough/cold (IJAMY 708).

**Caution:** Harmful in alcoholism, fainting, vomiting, tiredness, confusion, thirst, burning sensation, poisoning, Pitta-caused diseases, and blood-related conditions.

### Habit 9 — Warm water throughout the day

**Research:** Drinking hot water increased metabolism by 30%, lasting 30–40 min after consumption; facilitated weight loss, improved digestion, reduced constipation (Medical News Today 319673).

### Habit 10 — Laṅghana (fasting)

Fasting is one of 10 types of *laṅghana*. Skipping dinner **once a week** is ideal for all body types.

**Research:** Calorie restriction/fasting in overweight humans improves cardiac risk factors, insulin sensitivity, and reduces oxidative damage to DNA and RNA — promoting healthy aging (NCBI PMC5514430).

## Closing verse

> **भोजनान्ते पिबेत्तक्रं वासरान्ते पिबेत्पयः ।**
> **निशान्ते च पिबेद्वारि त्रिभिर्रोगो न जायते ॥**
>
> *bhojanānte pibet-takraṃ vāsarānte pibet-payaḥ ।*
> *niśānte ca pibed-vāri tribhir-rogo na jāyate ॥*

**Translation:** "By drinking **buttermilk at the end of a meal**, **milk at the end of the day**, and **water at the end of the night (i.e. in the morning)**, one does not get diseases."

## Ayurveda's highest goal

> **धर्म-अर्थ-काम-मोक्षाणां आरोग्यं मूलं उत्तमं।** — चरक संहिता सूत्र १।२४
> *dharma-artha-kāma-mokṣāṇāṃ ārogyaṃ mūlaṃ uttamaṃ*
> "Health is the supreme root of Dharma, Artha, Kāma, and Mokṣa."

- **Dharma** — duties/actions that give us support
- **Artha** — material wealth
- **Kāma** — our wishes
- **Mokṣa** — freedom from all attachments

All four have **health** as their common root.
