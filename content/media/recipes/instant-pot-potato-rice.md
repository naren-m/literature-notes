# Instant Pot Potato Rice

Make this simple potato rice recipe using an Instant Pot in just 30 minutes. The pilaf is good as a one-pot meal served with any curry or dal — kid-friendly lunchbox material.

## How to make it

- Prep the potatoes and fenugreek leaves. Skin can be left on; clean thoroughly.
- Soak the basmati rice for 15–30 minutes. Start by soaking; by the time you need it, it will be ready.
- Start the Instant Pot in sauté mode and add oil.
- Once the oil is hot, add bay leaf, black cumin seeds (shajeera), cardamom, and cloves.
- When they splutter, add sliced onions and chopped ginger; fry 3–4 minutes.
- Add potatoes; fry a couple of minutes.
- Add coconut milk, water, and salt to the inner pot.
- Drain the soaked rice; add the rice and chopped fenugreek leaves to the pot.
- Mix gently — don't overmix or rice grains will break.
- Close, seal the vent, and pressure-cook on **High pressure for 5 minutes**.
- Wait 5 minutes after the cook ends, then do a manual pressure release.
- Remove the inner pot; let it rest 10 minutes.
- Fluff with a spatula and serve.

## Recipe (as captured)

- 2 cups basmati rice
- 15 baby potatoes (around 200 g)
- 1.5 cups water
- 1 cup thick coconut milk
- ½ cup fenugreek leaves (methi)
- 1 small onion, sliced
- ½ tsp chopped ginger
- ½ tsp black cumin seeds (shajeera)
- bay leaf, cardamom, cloves to temper

## Notes and variations

- Can be made with regular cubed potatoes instead of baby potatoes.
- Add paneer, tofu, or sprouts for protein. With sprouts, use 1.5 cups water per cup of rice; rice-only needs ~1.25 cups water per cup of rice.
- Seasonal veggies (peas, sweet corn, mushrooms) work; add with the potatoes — no extra water needed.
- Lightly spiced — add green chili for heat.
- Dried fenugreek can sub for fresh.
- Mint and coriander leaves are good additions; stir in *after* pressure release so they don't overcook.
- Coconut milk lends a creamy, slightly sweet note. The recipe also works with thin coconut milk (2.5 cups, no water) or just water.
