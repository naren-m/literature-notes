**Attributed to Ādi Śaṅkarācārya**

First recitation/writing of [[AdiShankaraCharya]]

- ## Verse 1 (प्रथमः श्लोकः)
- ### Sanskrit Text
  
  **मनो बुद्धिः अहङ्कारः चित्तानि नाहं न च श्रोत्रजिह्वे न च घ्राणनेत्रे न च व्योम भूमिः न तेजो न वायुः चिदानन्दरूपः शिवोऽहं शिवोऽहम्**
  
  ---
- ### Word-by-Word Analysis
- #### **मनः (manaḥ)** — Mind
- **Root:** √मन् (man) — Dhātupāṭha 04.0073: "jñāne" (in knowing/thinking)
- **Formation:** मन् + असुन् (kṛt pratyaya) → मनस्
- **Grammatical Form:** Neuter, nominative singular
- **Meaning:** The faculty of deliberation, desire, and doubt; the seat of saṅkalpa (resolution) and vikalpa (alternatives)
- **Philosophical Note:** Sankalpatmika [[manas (సంకల్పాత్మిక మనస్) — the mind whose nature is to form intentions/resolutions. It is the internal organ that receives sense data and presents it to buddhi.
- #### **बुद्धिः (buddhiḥ)** — Intellect
- **Root:** √बुध् (budh) — Dhātupāṭha 04.0068: "avagamane" (in understanding/awakening)
- **Formation:** बुध् + क्तिन् (kṛt pratyaya) → बुद्धि
- **Grammatical Form:** Feminine, nominative singular
- **Meaning:** The determinative faculty; the power of judgment and decision
- **Philosophical Note:** Niścayātmikā buddhi (నిశ్చయాత్మక బుద్ధి) — intellect whose nature is certainty/determination. It determines "this is so" after manas deliberates.
- #### **अहङ्कारः (ahaṅkāraḥ)** — Ego-sense
- **Root:** अहम् + √कृ (kṛ) — "I-making"
- **Formation:** अहम् + कार (from √कṛ "to do") → अहङ्कार
- **Grammatical Form:** Masculine, nominative singular
- **Sandhi:** अहम् + कार → अहङ्कार (म् → ङ् before क्)
- **Meaning:** The sense of "I-ness"; the faculty that claims ownership ("I did this")
- **Philosophical Note:** As per your notes: "Neenu chesānu ani gurthu techukovaḍam ahaṅkāram" — the recognition "I did this."
- #### **चित्तानि (cittāni)** — Minds/Memory-stores
- **Root:** √चित् (cit) — Dhātupāṭha 01.0039: "saṃjñāne" (in consciousness/awareness)
- **Formation:** चित् + क्त (kṛt pratyaya) → चित्त
- **Grammatical Form:** Neuter, nominative plural
- **Meaning:** The storehouse of impressions (saṃskāras); memory faculty
- **Philosophical Note:** Chitta śuddhi = purification of the mind-stuff. "Chesina pani gurthu pettukunedi chittam" — that which remembers deeds done.
  
  **Note on Antaḥkaraṇa Catuṣṭaya (Inner Instrument Quartet):**
- Manas = Deliberation (ālocana)
- Buddhi = Determination (niścaya)
- Citta = Memory (smṛti/saṃskāra)
- Ahaṅkāra = Ego-identification (abhimāna)
- #### **न (na)** — Not
- **Type:** Avyaya (indeclinable), negation particle
- **Function:** Negates the identification with each element
- #### **अहम् (aham)** — I
- **Type:** First person singular pronoun, nominative
- **Philosophical Import:** The "I" that is being negated is the empirical self; the "I" that remains is pure consciousness
- #### **न च (na ca)** — And not
- **च (ca):** Conjunctive particle meaning "and"
- **Combined:** "nor," "and not"
- #### **श्रोत्रजिह्वे (śrotra-jihve)** — Ears and tongue (hearing and taste)
  
  **श्रोत्रम् (śrotram):**
- **Root:** √श्रु (śru) — "to hear"
- **Formation:** श्रु + त्र (karmaṇi pratyaya) → श्रोत्र (guṇa: u → o)
- **Meaning:** The organ/sense of hearing
  
  **जिह्वा (jihvā):**
- **Root:** √जुह्/√हु (hu/juh) — connected to licking/tasting
- **Formation:** Irregular nominal formation
- **Meaning:** Tongue; the organ of taste
- **Grammatical Form:** Dvandva compound, neuter dual (dual for pair of organs)
- #### **घ्राणनेत्रे (ghrāṇa-netre)** — Nose and eyes (smell and sight)
  
  **घ्राणम् (ghrāṇam):**
- **Root:** √घ्रा (ghrā) — "to smell"
- **Formation:** घ्रा + ण (lyuṭ pratyaya) → घ्राण
- **Meaning:** The organ/sense of smell
  
  **नेत्रम् (netram):**
- **Root:** √नी (nī) — "to lead"
- **Formation:** नी + त्र (karaṇa pratyaya) → नेत्र
- **Meaning:** "That which leads" — the eye guides/leads perception
- **Grammatical Form:** Dvandva compound, neuter dual
  
  **Note:** The five sense organs (pañca jñānendriyas) are śrotra (hearing), tvak (touch), cakṣus (sight), rasanā/jihvā (taste), ghrāṇa (smell). Only four are mentioned here; tvak (skin/touch) is implied.
- #### **व्योम (vyoma)** — Space/Sky
- **Root:** √वी (vī) — "to pervade" + man suffix → vyoman
- **Formation:** Alternatively from vi + √अव् (av) meaning "covering"
- **Grammatical Form:** Neuter, nominative singular
- **Meaning:** Ākāśa; the element of space/ether
- **Philosophical Note:** First of the pañcabhūtas (five elements); most subtle; characterized by śabda (sound)
- #### **भूमिः (bhūmiḥ)** — Earth
- **Root:** √भू (bhū) — Dhātupāṭha 01.0001: "sattāyām" (in being/existence)
- **Formation:** भू + मि (uṇādi pratyaya) → भूमि
- **Grammatical Form:** Feminine, nominative singular
- **Meaning:** Earth; the element characterized by gandha (smell) and solidity
- **Philosophical Note:** Grossest of the five elements; last in manifestation
- #### **तेजः (tejaḥ)** — Fire/Light
- **Root:** √तिज् (tij) — "to sharpen, to be sharp"
- **Formation:** तिज् + असुन् → तेजस् (guṇa: i → e)
- **Grammatical Form:** Neuter, nominative singular
- **Meaning:** Fire; the element of light and heat; characterized by rūpa (form/visibility)
- #### **वायुः (vāyuḥ)** — Air/Wind
- **Root:** √वा (vā) — "to blow"
- **Formation:** वा + उ (uṇādi pratyaya) → वायु
- **Grammatical Form:** Masculine, nominative singular
- **Meaning:** Air; the element characterized by sparśa (touch)
  
  **Note on Pañcabhūtas (Five Elements):**
  
  1. Ākāśa/Vyoma (Space) — sound
  2. Vāyu (Air) — touch
  3. Tejas/Agni (Fire) — form
  4. Āpas/Jala (Water) — taste [not mentioned explicitly here]
  5. Pṛthivī/Bhūmi (Earth) — smell
- #### **चिदानन्दरूपः (cid-ānanda-rūpaḥ)** — Of the form of consciousness-bliss
  
  **चित् (cit):**
- **Root:** √चित् (cit) — Dhātupāṭha 01.0039: "saṃjñāne" (in consciousness)
- **Meaning:** Pure awareness; consciousness as such
  
  **आनन्द (ānanda):**
- **Root:** √नन्द् (nand) — "to rejoice"
- **Formation:** आ + नन्द् + घञ् → आनन्द
- **Meaning:** Bliss; not ordinary happiness but unconditioned joy
  
  **रूपः (rūpaḥ):**
- **Root:** √रूप् (rūp) — "to form"
- **Formation:** रूप + अ → रूप
- **Meaning:** Form, nature, essence
- **Compound Type:** Bahuvrīhi — "one whose nature/form is consciousness-bliss"
- **Grammatical Form:** Masculine, nominative singular (agreeing with implicit "I")
- #### **शिवः अहम् (śivaḥ aham)** — I am Śiva
  
  **शिवः (śivaḥ):**
- **Root:** √शी (śī) — "to rest/lie down" or √शिव् (śiv) — "to be auspicious"
- **Meaning:** The auspicious one; ultimate reality; pure consciousness
- **Amarakośa Reference:** "शिवा गौरी..." (3.3.795) — Śiva denotes the auspicious
- **Philosophical Import:** Not just the deity, but the state of being that is pure, auspicious, unchanging
  
  **शिवोऽहम् (śivo'ham):**
- **Sandhi:** शिवः + अहम् → शिवोऽहम् (visarga sandhi: ḥ + a → o')
- **Meaning:** "I am Śiva" — the mahāvākya-like declaration of identity with the absolute

- ## Verse 2 (द्वितीयः श्लोकः)
- ### Sanskrit Text
  
  **न च प्राणसंज्ञो न वै पञ्चवायुः न वा सप्तधातुः न वा पञ्चकोशः न वाक्पाणिपादं न चोपस्थपायु चिदानन्दरूपः शिवोऽहं शिवोऽहम्**
  
  ---
- ### Word-by-Word Analysis
- #### **प्राणसंज्ञः (prāṇa-saṃjñaḥ)** — Known as/identified with Prāṇa
  
  **प्राण (prāṇa):**
- **Root:** प्र + √अन् (an) — Dhātupāṭha 04.0071/0072: "prāṇane" (in breathing/living)
- **Formation:** प्र + अन् + अ → प्राण
- **Meaning:** Life-breath; vital force; specifically the forward-moving breath
  
  **संज्ञा (saṃjñā):**
- **Root:** सम् + √ज्ञा (jñā) — "to know together"
- **Formation:** सम् + ज्ञा + अ → संज्ञा
- **Meaning:** Name, designation, consciousness
- **Compound Type:** Bahuvrīhi — "one whose name/identity is prāṇa"
- #### **पञ्चवायुः (pañca-vāyuḥ)** — The Five Vital Airs
  
  **पञ्च (pañca):** Five (cardinal number)
  
  **वायुः (vāyuḥ):** Air/vital breath (as analyzed above)
  
  **The Pañca Vāyus:**
  
  1. **प्राण (prāṇa)** — Forward breath; respiration; seated in chest
  2. **अपान (apāna)** — Downward breath; excretion; seated in lower abdomen
  3. **व्यान (vyāna)** — Pervading breath; circulation; throughout body
  4. **उदान (udāna)** — Upward breath; speech/death; seated in throat
  5. **समान (samāna)** — Equalizing breath; digestion; seated in navel
- #### **सप्तधातुः (sapta-dhātuḥ)** — The Seven Bodily Constituents
  
  **सप्त (sapta):** Seven (cardinal number)
  
  **धातुः (dhātuḥ):**
- **Root:** √धा (dhā) — Dhātupāṭha: "dhāraṇe" (in holding/supporting)
- **Formation:** धा + तु (uṇādi) → धातु
- **Meaning:** Element, constituent, that which holds/supports the body
  
  **The Sapta Dhātus (Āyurvedic):**
  
  1. **रस (rasa)** — Plasma/lymph/chyle
  2. **रक्त (rakta)** — Blood
  3. **मांस (māṃsa)** — Muscle tissue
  4. **मेद (meda)** — Fat/adipose tissue
  5. **अस्थि (asthi)** — Bone
  6. **मज्जा (majjā)** — Marrow
  7. **शुक्र (śukra)** — Reproductive tissue
- #### **पञ्चकोशः (pañca-kośaḥ)** — The Five Sheaths
  
  **कोश (kośa):**
- **Root:** √कुश् (kuś) — "to embrace, contain"
- **Formation:** कुश् + अ → कोश
- **Meaning:** Sheath, covering, container
  
  **The Pañca Kośas (from Taittirīya Upaniṣad):**
  
  1. **अन्नमयकोश (annamaya-kośa)** — Food/physical sheath
  2. **प्राणमयकोश (prāṇamaya-kośa)** — Vital-breath sheath
  3. **मनोमयकोश (manomaya-kośa)** — Mental sheath
  4. **विज्ञानमयकोश (vijñānamaya-kośa)** — Intellectual/wisdom sheath
  5. **आनन्दमयकोश (ānandamaya-kośa)** — Bliss sheath
- #### **वाक् (vāk)** — Speech
- **Root:** √वच् (vac) — "to speak"
- **Formation:** वच् + क्विप् → वाच् → वाक् (shortened)
- **Meaning:** The organ of speech; one of the five karmendriyas
- #### **पाणि (pāṇi)** — Hands
- **Root:** √पण् (paṇ) — "to trade, deal"
- **Formation:** पण् + इ → पाणि (vṛddhi: a → ā)
- **Meaning:** Hands; organs of grasping; karmendriya
- #### **पादम् (pādam)** — Feet
- **Root:** √पद् (pad) — Dhātupāṭha 04.0065: "gatau" (in going)
- **Formation:** पद् + अ → पाद
- **Meaning:** Feet; organs of locomotion; karmendriya
- #### **उपस्थ (upastha)** — Generative organ
- **Root:** उप + √स्था (sthā) — "to stand near/below"
- **Formation:** उप + स्था + अ → उपस्थ
- **Meaning:** The organ of generation/procreation; karmendriya
- #### **पायु (pāyu)** — Excretory organ
- **Root:** √पा (pā) — "to protect/purify" (in the sense of expelling impurities)
- **Formation:** पा + उ → पायु
- **Meaning:** The organ of excretion; anus; karmendriya
  
  **Note on Pañca Karmendriyas (Five Organs of Action):**
  
  1. Vāk — Speech
  2. Pāṇi — Hands (grasping)
  3. Pāda — Feet (locomotion)
  4. Upastha — Generative organ (procreation)
  5. Pāyu — Excretory organ (elimination)

- ## Verse 3 (तृतीयः श्लोकः)
- ### Sanskrit Text
  
  **न मे द्वेषरागौ न मे लोभमोहौ मदो नैव मे नैव मात्सर्यभावः न धर्मो न चार्थो न कामो न मोक्षः चिदानन्दरूपः शिवोऽहं शिवोऽहम्**
  
  ---
- ### Word-by-Word Analysis
- #### **मे (me)** — To me/of me
- **Type:** Dative/genitive singular of अस्मद् (first person pronoun)
- **Usage:** Here indicating possession or relationship
- #### **द्वेषरागौ (dveṣa-rāgau)** — Hatred and attachment
  
  **द्वेष (dveṣa):**
- **Root:** √द्विष् (dviṣ) — "to hate"
- **Formation:** द्विष् + घञ् → द्वेष (guṇa: i → e)
- **Meaning:** Hatred, aversion, dislike
  
  **राग (rāga):**
- **Root:** √रञ्ज् (rañj) — Dhātupāṭha 04.0063: "rāge" (in coloring/attachment)
- **Formation:** रञ्ज् + घञ् → राग
- **Meaning:** Attachment, passion, coloring (of the mind toward objects)
- **Grammatical Form:** Dvandva compound, masculine dual
- #### **लोभमोहौ (lobha-mohau)** — Greed and delusion
  
  **लोभ (lobha):**
- **Root:** √लुभ् (lubh) — "to desire, covet"
- **Formation:** लुभ् + घञ् → लोभ (guṇa: u → o)
- **Meaning:** Greed, avarice, covetousness
  
  **मोह (moha):**
- **Root:** √मुह् (muh) — "to be deluded"
- **Formation:** मुह् + घञ् → मोह (guṇa: u → o)
- **Meaning:** Delusion, confusion, ignorance
- **Grammatical Form:** Dvandva compound, masculine dual
- #### **मद (mada)** — Pride/intoxication
- **Root:** √मद् (mad) — Dhātupāṭha 01.0013: "stuti-moda-mada-svapna-kānti-gatiṣu"
- **Formation:** मद् + अ → मद
- **Meaning:** Pride, arrogance, intoxication (of ego)
- #### **मात्सर्य (mātsarya)** — Jealousy/envy
- **Root:** मत्सर (matsara) — "jealous person" + ञ्य → मात्सर्य
- **Formation:** Derived from matsara (which itself relates to √mad or competition)
- **Meaning:** Jealousy, envy, malice
  
  **भाव (bhāva):**
- **Root:** √भू (bhū) — "to be"
- **Formation:** भू + घञ् → भाव
- **Meaning:** State, feeling, disposition
  
  **Note on Ṣaḍripus (Six Enemies):**
  
  1. Kāma — Desire/lust
  2. Krodha — Anger
  3. Lobha — Greed
  4. Moha — Delusion
  5. Mada — Pride
  6. Mātsarya — Jealousy
- #### **धर्मः (dharmaḥ)** — Righteousness/Duty
- **Root:** √धृ (dhṛ) — "to hold, support"
- **Formation:** धृ + मन् → धर्म
- **Meaning:** That which upholds; religious duty; moral order; first puruṣārtha
- #### **अर्थः (arthaḥ)** — Wealth/Purpose
- **Root:** √ऋ (ṛ) — "to go, obtain"
- **Formation:** ऋ + थ → अर्थ
- **Meaning:** Wealth, material prosperity, meaning; second puruṣārtha
- #### **कामः (kāmaḥ)** — Desire
- **Root:** √कम् (kam) — "to desire, love"
- **Formation:** कम् + घञ् → काम
- **Meaning:** Desire, pleasure, love; third puruṣārtha
- #### **मोक्षः (mokṣaḥ)** — Liberation
- **Root:** √मुच् (muc) — "to release, free"
- **Formation:** मुच् + घञ् → मोक्ष (guṇa: u → o)
- **Meaning:** Liberation, final release from saṃsāra; fourth puruṣārtha
  
  **Note on Puruṣārthas (Four Goals of Human Life):**
  
  1. Dharma — Righteous conduct
  2. Artha — Material prosperity
  3. Kāma — Pleasure and desire
  4. Mokṣa — Liberation
  
  The negation of all four indicates transcendence of even the highest spiritual goal (mokṣa) because the Self was never bound to need liberation.

- ## Verse 4 (चतुर्थः श्लोकः)
- ### Sanskrit Text
  
  **न पुण्यं न पापं न सौख्यं न दुःखं न मन्त्रो न तीर्थं न वेदा न यज्ञाः अहं भोजनं नैव भोज्यं न भोक्ता चिदानन्दरूपः शिवोऽहं शिवोऽहम्**
  
  ---
- ### Word-by-Word Analysis
- #### **पुण्यम् (puṇyam)** — Merit/Virtue
- **Root:** √पुण् (puṇ) — "to be pure, virtuous"
- **Formation:** पुण् + य → पुण्य
- **Meaning:** Meritorious deed; positive karma; virtue
- #### **पापम् (pāpam)** — Sin/Demerit
- **Root:** √पा (pā) — "to fall" (in the sense of falling from grace)
- **Formation:** Connected to "falling"; पाप + अ
- **Meaning:** Sin; negative karma; evil deed
- #### **सौख्यम् (saukhyam)** — Happiness
- **Root:** सुख (sukha) — "happiness" + ष्यञ् → सौख्य
- **Formation:** Derived from sukha (su + kha = "good axle-hole" → comfortable)
- **Meaning:** State of happiness; pleasantness
- #### **दुःखम् (duḥkham)** — Suffering
- **Root:** दुस् + ख (kha) — "bad axle-hole" → discomfort
- **Formation:** दुस् + ख → दुःख
- **Meaning:** Suffering, pain, misery
- #### **मन्त्रः (mantraḥ)** — Sacred formula
- **Root:** √मन् (man) — "to think" + त्र (karaṇa pratyaya)
- **Formation:** मन् + त्र → मन्त्र
- **Meaning:** That which is used for thinking/meditation; sacred verse; hymn
- #### **तीर्थम् (tīrtham)** — Pilgrimage place
- **Root:** √तॄ (tṝ) — "to cross over"
- **Formation:** तॄ + थ → तीर्थ
- **Meaning:** Ford; crossing place; pilgrimage site that helps one "cross over" to liberation
- #### **वेदाः (vedāḥ)** — The Vedas
- **Root:** √विद् (vid) — "to know"
- **Formation:** विद् + घञ् → वेद
- **Grammatical Form:** Masculine, nominative plural
- **Meaning:** Knowledge; the four sacred texts (Ṛg, Yajur, Sāma, Atharva)
- #### **यज्ञाः (yajñāḥ)** — Sacrifices
- **Root:** √यज् (yaj) — "to worship, sacrifice"
- **Formation:** यज् + ञ → यज्ञ
- **Grammatical Form:** Masculine, nominative plural
- **Meaning:** Ritual sacrifices; worship ceremonies
- #### **भोजनम् (bhojanam)** — Food/Experience
- **Root:** √भुज् (bhuj) — "to enjoy, experience"
- **Formation:** भुज् + ल्युट् → भोजन (guṇa: u → o)
- **Meaning:** Food; that which is experienced; the act of experiencing
- #### **भोज्यम् (bhojyam)** — That which is to be experienced
- **Root:** √भुज् (bhuj) — "to enjoy"
- **Formation:** भुज् + यत् (karmavacanīya) → भोज्य
- **Meaning:** The object of experience; what is eaten/enjoyed
- #### **भोक्ता (bhoktā)** — The experiencer
- **Root:** √भुज् (bhuj) — "to enjoy"
- **Formation:** भुज् + तृच् → भोक्तृ → भोक्ता (nominative singular)
- **Meaning:** The subject who experiences; the enjoyer
  
  **Note on the Tripuṭī (Triad):**
- Bhojanam — the act/instrument of experience
- Bhojyam — the object of experience
- Bhoktā — the subject/experiencer
  
  This verse negates the entire experiential triad (tripuṭī), indicating that the Self transcends the subject-object-action distinction.

- ## Verse 5 (पञ्चमः श्लोकः)
- ### Sanskrit Text
  
  **न मे मृत्युशङ्का न मे जातिभेदः पिता नैव मे नैव माता न जन्म न बन्धुः न मित्रं गुरुः नैव शिष्यः चिदानन्दरूपः शिवोऽहं शिवोऽहम्**
  
  ---
- ### Word-by-Word Analysis
- #### **मृत्युशङ्का (mṛtyu-śaṅkā)** — Fear of death
  
  **मृत्यु (mṛtyu):**
- **Root:** √मृ (mṛ) — "to die"
- **Formation:** मृ + त्यु (uṇādi) → मृत्यु
- **Meaning:** Death
  
  **शङ्का (śaṅkā):**
- **Root:** √शङ्क् (śaṅk) — "to doubt, fear"
- **Formation:** शङ्क् + अ → शङ्का
- **Meaning:** Fear, doubt, apprehension
- #### **जातिभेदः (jāti-bhedaḥ)** — Distinction of caste/birth
  
  **जाति (jāti):**
- **Root:** √जन् (jan) — Dhātupāṭha 04.0044: "prādurbhāve" (in being born)
- **Formation:** जन् + क्तिन् → जाति
- **Meaning:** Birth, species, caste, class
  
  **भेद (bheda):**
- **Root:** √भिद् (bhid) — "to split, divide"
- **Formation:** भिद् + घञ् → भेद (guṇa: i → e)
- **Meaning:** Division, difference, distinction
- #### **पिता (pitā)** — Father
- **Root:** √पा (pā) — "to protect"
- **Formation:** पा + तृ → पितृ → पिता (nominative singular)
- **Meaning:** Father; protector
- #### **माता (mātā)** — Mother
- **Root:** √मा (mā) — "to measure, create"
- **Formation:** मा + तृ → मातृ → माता (nominative singular)
- **Meaning:** Mother; creator
- #### **जन्म (janma)** — Birth
- **Root:** √जन् (jan) — "to be born"
- **Formation:** जन् + मन् → जन्म
- **Meaning:** Birth; coming into existence
- #### **बन्धुः (bandhuḥ)** — Kinsman/Relative
- **Root:** √बन्ध् (bandh) — "to bind"
- **Formation:** बन्ध् + उ → बन्धु
- **Meaning:** One bound by relationship; relative; kinsman
- #### **मित्रम् (mitram)** — Friend
- **Root:** √मिद् (mid) — "to love, be fond of"
- **Formation:** मिद् + त्र → मित्र
- **Meaning:** Friend; one who loves
- #### **गुरुः (guruḥ)** — Teacher
- **Root:** √गॄ (gṝ) — "to praise" or from गुरु as "heavy/weighty"
- **Formation:** गुरु (inherent adjective form) — "weighty, venerable"
- **Meaning:** Spiritual teacher; one who removes darkness (gu = darkness, ru = remover)
- #### **शिष्यः (śiṣyaḥ)** — Student
- **Root:** √शास् (śās) — "to instruct"
- **Formation:** शास् + यत् → शिष्य (with samprasāraṇa: ā → i)
- **Meaning:** One to be instructed; student; disciple

- ## Verse 6 (षष्ठः श्लोकः)
- ### Sanskrit Text
  
  **अहं निर्विकल्पो निराकाररूपो विभुत्वाच्च सर्वत्र सर्वेन्द्रियाणाम् न चासङ्गतं नैव मुक्तिः न मेयः चिदानन्दरूपः शिवोऽहं शिवोऽहम्**
  
  ---
- ### Word-by-Word Analysis
- #### **निर्विकल्पः (nirvikalpaḥ)** — Free from mental modifications
- **Formation:** निस् + विकल्प → निर्विकल्प
- **निस् (nis):** Prefix meaning "without, free from"
  
  **विकल्प (vikalpa):**
- **Root:** वि + √कॢप् (kḷp) — "to be fit, imagine"
- **Formation:** वि + कॢप् + घञ् → विकल्प
- **Meaning:** Mental modification; conceptual thought; alternative thinking
- **Compound Meaning:** One free from all conceptual modifications; beyond thought constructs
- #### **निराकाररूपः (nirākāra-rūpaḥ)** — Of formless form
  
  **निराकार (nirākāra):**
- **Formation:** निस् + आकार → निराकार
- **आकार (ākāra):** From आ + √कृ (kṛ) — "form, shape"
- **Meaning:** Formless; without shape
  
  **रूपः (rūpaḥ):** Form, nature (as analyzed above)
- **Compound Type:** Bahuvrīhi — "one whose form is formlessness"
- **Philosophical Note:** A beautiful paradox — having formlessness as one's form indicates the ineffable nature of the Self
- #### **विभुत्वात् (vibhutvāt)** — Due to all-pervasiveness
- **Formation:** विभु + त्व + आत् (ablative)
  
  **विभु (vibhu):**
- **Root:** वि + √भू (bhū) — "to be everywhere"
- **Formation:** वि + भू + उ → विभु
- **Meaning:** All-pervading; omnipresent
  
  **-त्वात् (-tvāt):** Abstract noun suffix "ness" in ablative = "because of"
- **Meaning:** "Because of all-pervasiveness"
- #### **सर्वत्र (sarvatra)** — Everywhere
- **Formation:** सर्व + त्र (locative suffix)
- **Meaning:** In all places; everywhere
- #### **सर्वेन्द्रियाणाम् (sarvendriyāṇām)** — Of all sense organs
- **Formation:** सर्व + इन्द्रिय → सर्वेन्द्रिय (sandhi: a + i → e)
- **Grammatical Form:** Genitive plural
  
  **इन्द्रिय (indriya):**
- **Root:** इन्द्र (Indra) + य → इन्द्रिय
- **Meaning:** Power, faculty; sense organ (named after Indra, lord of senses)
- **Includes:** Both jñānendriyas (sense organs) and karmendriyas (organs of action)
- #### **असङ्गतम् (asaṅgatam)** — Non-attachment
- **Formation:** अ + सम् + √गम् (gam) — "not going together"
- **Alternatively:** अ + सङ्ग + त — "without attachment"
  
  **सङ्ग (saṅga):**
- **Root:** सम् + √ञ्ज् (sañj) — "to cling"
- **Meaning:** Attachment, association
- **Meaning:** Neither attached nor unattached; beyond the duality of attachment
- #### **मुक्तिः (muktiḥ)** — Liberation
- **Root:** √मुच् (muc) — "to release"
- **Formation:** मुच् + क्तिन् → मुक्ति
- **Meaning:** Liberation, release
- **Philosophical Note:** Even liberation is negated because the Self was never bound
- #### **मेयः (meyaḥ)** — Measurable/Knowable
- **Root:** √मा (mā) — Dhātupāṭha 04.0037: "māne" (in measuring)
- **Formation:** मा + य (karmavacanīya) → मेय
- **Meaning:** That which can be measured; the knowable object
- **Philosophical Note:** The Self cannot be known as an object because it is the eternal subject (knower)

- ## Summary of Key Philosophical Concepts
- ### 1. The Negation Method (Neti-Neti)
  
  Each verse systematically negates identification with:
- **Verse 1:** Antaḥkaraṇa (inner instruments) and sense organs; elements
- **Verse 2:** Vital airs, bodily constituents, sheaths, and organs of action
- **Verse 3:** Emotional afflictions and life goals
- **Verse 4:** Religious practices and the experiential triad
- **Verse 5:** Social relations and distinctions
- **Verse 6:** All conceptual categories
- ### 2. The Affirmation
  
  After each negation: **चिदानन्दरूपः शिवोऽहम्** — "I am of the nature of consciousness-bliss, I am Shiva"
  
  This combines:
- **चित् (cit)** — Pure awareness (from √cit "to be conscious")
- **आनन्द (ānanda)** — Unconditioned bliss (from √nand "to rejoice")
- **शिव (śiva)** — The auspicious, pure, unchanging reality
- ### 3. The Chathustaya (Quartet) Framework
  
  As referenced in your notes from Garikapati Narasimha Rao:
