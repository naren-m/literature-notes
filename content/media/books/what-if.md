# What If? — Randall Munroe

Randall Munroe's *What If?: Serious Scientific Answers to Absurd Hypothetical Questions* (2014). Spin-off from Munroe's xkcd blog of the same name. "Want to read" status — no personal notes yet.

## Why it's on the list

Each chapter takes an absurd reader question ("What would happen if you tried to hit a baseball pitched at 90% the speed of light?") and answers it with first-principles physics + back-of-the-envelope estimates, with stick-figure illustrations. Useful as:

- A **Fermi-estimation exerciser** — every chapter is a modelled estimation problem.
- A **light-touch physics refresher** — orders of magnitude, energy densities, scaling laws.

Munroe's tone is deliberately disarming; the book's real skill is Fermi thinking under an entertainment wrapper.

## Open threads

- When read, pull a handful of the estimation techniques into a "Fermi estimation" research note.
- Pair with a more rigorous intro to estimation (e.g. Weinstein & Adam's *Guesstimation*).
