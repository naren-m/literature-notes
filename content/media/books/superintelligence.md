# Superintelligence — Nick Bostrom

Nick Bostrom's *Superintelligence: Paths, Dangers, Strategies* (2014). "Want to read" status — no personal notes yet.

## Why it's on the list

Bostrom's book (with Stuart Russell's *Human Compatible* and Christian's *The Alignment Problem*) is one of the canonical pre-GPT formal treatments of AI risk. Written before the transformer era, but its core arguments about instrumental convergence, orthogonality, and the control problem are now standard vocabulary in the alignment field.

## Concepts to expect

- **Paths to superintelligence** — AI, whole brain emulation, biological cognition, brain-computer interfaces, networks.
- **Kinetics of takeoff** — slow vs. fast vs. moderate; the "intelligence explosion" idea.
- **The orthogonality thesis** — intelligence and final goals are orthogonal; a superintelligence can pursue almost any goal.
- **Instrumental convergence** — almost any final goal produces a small set of convergent sub-goals (self-preservation, resource acquisition, goal-preservation).
- **The control problem** — capability control (boxing, tripwires) vs. motivation selection (direct specification, indirect normativity, coherent extrapolated volition).
