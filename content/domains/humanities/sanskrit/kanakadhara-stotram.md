## About the Kanakadhara Stotram

The **Kanakadhara Stotram** is one of the most beloved hymns to **Goddess Lakshmi**, composed by **Ādi Śaṅkara** (8th c. CE). The title means "The Hymn of the Shower of Gold" — *kanaka* "gold", *dhārā* "stream, shower".

### The legend of its composition

Traditional hagiography places the composition of the hymn during Ādi Śaṅkara's *bhikṣā* (alms-begging) period as a young sannyāsī. Visiting the house of a desperately poor Brahmin family, he found them with nothing to offer except a single dry gooseberry (*āmalakī*) — which the woman of the house gave to him as a token of her devotion. Moved by her selfless generosity in the face of poverty, Śaṅkara spontaneously composed the 21 (some traditions 22) verses of the Kanakadhara Stotram, invoking Lakshmi to grant the household prosperity. Lakshmi responded by showering golden gooseberries onto the house — hence *Kanakadhārā*, "stream of gold".

### Structure

- **21–22 verses** in various Sanskrit metres (primarily *vasanta-tilakā* and *mālinī*).
- Each verse praises a different aspect of Lakshmi: her beauty, her relationship to Viṣṇu (as his consort), her association with the lotus, her role as the giver of wealth and sustenance.
- Traditionally chanted on **Fridays** (the day of Venus/Śukra, associated with Lakshmi), and during **Akṣaya Tṛtīyā**, **Lakṣmī Pūjā** on Diwali, and the autumn **Navarātri**.
- Considered one of the most auspicious hymns for the removal of financial difficulty.

### Opening verse (traditional)

> **वन्दे वन्द्यारुणश्रीचरणकमलमासाद्य मुक्त्यै समृद्ध्यै ।**
> *vande vandyāruṇa-śrī-caraṇa-kamalam āsādya muktyai samṛddhyai*

(Variants exist across editions.)

### Famous verses

- *"Aṅgaṁ hareḥ pulakabhūṣaṇam āśrayantī..."* — describing Lakshmi as the ornament of Viṣṇu's body, causing his horripilation (*pulaka*).
- *"Hrīṅkārādyā lalitā-pada-pāvanā kathā..."* — invoking her Bīja mantra *Hrīṁ*.
- Closing verses in the *śrī-sūkta* style enumerating her many names.
