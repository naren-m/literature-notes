# Sun

## Basic facts

- **Type**: G2V main-sequence yellow dwarf star.
- **Age**: ~4.6 billion years, roughly halfway through its ~10-billion-year main-sequence lifetime.
- **Mass**: `1.989 × 10³⁰ kg` — about **333,000 × Earth's mass**, and contains ~99.86% of the Solar System's total mass.
- **Radius**: `~696,000 km` — ~109 × Earth's radius.
- **Distance from Earth**: `~1 AU = 149.6 million km`. Light takes about 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach us.
- **Surface temperature**: ~5,778 K (photosphere).
- **Core temperature**: ~15 million K.
- **Composition**: ~73% hydrogen, ~25% helium, traces of heavier elements.

## How it shines

The Sun produces energy via **proton-proton fusion** in its core:

```
4 H → He + 2 e⁺ + 2 νₑ + energy
```

Each second, the Sun converts roughly 600 million tonnes of hydrogen into helium, releasing the mass difference as energy according to `E = mc²`. Only a tiny fraction of the Sun's mass is converted per unit time — but it's enough to power the Solar System for billions of years.

## Layered structure (from inside out)

- **Core** — fusion happens here.
- **Radiative zone** — energy diffuses outward as photons random-walking through dense plasma. A photon generated in the core takes an estimated 100,000+ years to reach the surface.
- **Convective zone** — plasma boils up, cools, sinks back. Where sunspots, granulation, and magnetic activity are driven.
- **Photosphere** — the visible "surface." Where light we see actually escapes.
- **Chromosphere** and **corona** — outer atmospheric layers. Corona is paradoxically millions of degrees hotter than the photosphere — the "coronal heating problem."
- **Heliosphere** — the solar wind extends the Sun's influence out past Pluto.

## Observed phenomena

- **Sunspots** — cooler regions tied to strong magnetic fields; follow an ~11-year cycle.
- **Solar flares and coronal mass ejections** — magnetically driven eruptions that can disrupt Earth's magnetosphere and satellites.
- **Solar wind** — continuous outflow of charged particles, carves out the heliosphere.
- **Differential rotation** — the Sun rotates faster at its equator (~25 days) than its poles (~35 days), because it's not a rigid body.

## Life cycle (future)

- Hydrogen in the core will run out in ~5 billion years.
- Sun will expand into a **red giant**, swallowing Mercury and Venus, possibly Earth.
- Outer layers eject as a **planetary nebula**.
- Core collapses into a **white dwarf** the size of Earth, gradually cooling over trillions of years.
