# When Breath Becomes Air — Paul Kalanithi

Paul Kalanithi's *When Breath Becomes Air* (2016, posthumous). Status: "Next up" — bookmarked for reading, no personal notes yet.

## Why it's on the list

Kalanithi was a Stanford neurosurgery resident, on track to finish training and start as faculty, when he was diagnosed with stage-IV metastatic lung cancer at 36. He wrote the book during the last year of his life; it was completed and published posthumously by his wife Lucy.

The book is uncommon in that it is both:
- A **neurosurgeon's reflection on the practice of medicine** — what it means to confront mortality as the doctor.
- A **patient's reflection on being a patient** — a near-overnight role reversal after the diagnosis.

Themes: meaning-under-finitude, the difference between clinical and lived knowledge of death, the role of the humanities in medicine (Kalanithi studied English literature before medicine), parenthood-as-legacy.
