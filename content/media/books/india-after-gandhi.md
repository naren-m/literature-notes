# India after Gandhi — Guha

Ramachandra Guha's *India after Gandhi: The History of the World's Largest Democracy* (2007; revised editions 2017, 2023). "Want to read" status — no personal notes yet.

## Why it's on the list

The standard single-volume political-social history of post-independence India. Most histories of India end at Partition; Guha picks up in 1947 and runs through the late 2000s, covering:

- **The Nehru era** — integration of princely states, Constitution, Hindi-vs-English linguistic politics, non-alignment.
- **Reorganisation on linguistic lines** (1956) and its ongoing aftermath.
- **Wars**: 1962 (China), 1965 & 1971 (Pakistan), Kargil (1999).
- **Indira Gandhi, the Emergency, and the Janata interlude.**
- **Economic liberalisation** (1991) and the post-liberalisation middle class.
- **Religious and caste politics** — Ayodhya, Mandal, the rise of the BJP.
- **The Indian state as a working, if imperfect, democracy** in a region where democracy has largely failed.
