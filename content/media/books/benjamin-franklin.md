# Benjamin Franklin — Isaacson

Walter Isaacson's *Benjamin Franklin: An American Life* (2003). Status: "Next up" — bookmarked for reading, no personal notes yet.

## Why it's on the list

Isaacson's Franklin biography is the most widely-recommended single-volume treatment of Franklin — covering the scientist, printer, postmaster, diplomat, writer of *Poor Richard's Almanack*, and one of America's most-quoted pragmatists. Relevant to the vault for:

- **Self-development**: Franklin's 13 Virtues exercise (temperance, silence, order, resolution, frugality, industry, sincerity, justice, moderation, cleanliness, tranquility, chastity, humility) — one of the earliest structured habit-tracking systems in Western literature. Pairs with [[atomic-habits]] and [[1-percent-better-every-day]].
- **Writing craft**: Franklin's method for learning to write — rebuilding essays from memory and comparing — is one of the earliest documented deliberate-practice regimens.
- **Civic entrepreneurship**: the Library Company, the Junto, the University of Pennsylvania, the first fire brigade.
