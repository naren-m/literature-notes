# Instant Pot Vegetarian Chili

Sweet potato + quinoa + beans chili made entirely in the Instant Pot. ~8 minutes pressure cooking + 10 minutes to come to pressure. Easily made vegan by skipping dairy toppings.

## Ingredients

- **Sweet potatoes** — natural sweetness pairs with chili spices.
- **Red bell peppers** — extra sweetness and a hidden serving of veg.
- **Onion + garlic** — chili foundation.
- **Spices** — chili powder, cumin (both essential) plus chipotle chili powder for smoky depth. Up the chipotle (or add cayenne) for heat.
- **Vegetable broth** — keeps it vegetarian; chicken broth works too if not strict.
- **Tomato sauce** — low- or no-salt-added for thick texture without too much sodium.
- **Quinoa** — thickens and absorbs tomato flavor as it cooks.
- **Beans** — kidney + black is the default mix; pinto or white work too.
- **Sugar** — small amount to balance tomato acidity (optional).
- **Toppings** — Greek yogurt, avocado, chips, cheese, cilantro.

## Method

1. Sauté the onion in the Instant Pot until soft. Add potatoes, pepper, garlic, and spices; cook until fragrant.
2. Add half the broth, scraping the fond off the bottom. Stir in the rest of the broth and the quinoa. Pour the tomato sauce on top and **do not stir**.
3. Cover, seal, and pressure-cook on Manual/High **for 8 minutes**. Quick-release immediately.
4. Stir in the beans and sugar. Close the lid for a few minutes to let it thicken. Serve hot with toppings.

## Storage

- **Fridge:** 5 days in an airtight container.
- **Freezer:** up to 3 months.
- **Reheat:** stovetop on medium with a splash of broth/water if too thick.
- **Make ahead:** chop sweet potatoes, peppers, garlic, onions up to 1 day in advance.

## Pairings

- Salads: arugula salad or shaved Brussels Caesar.
- Bread: cornbread (Mexican-style) for dunking.
- Veggies: roasted broccoli, roasted Brussels sprouts.

## Health notes

High in fiber, vitamins, and minerals (sweet potatoes, peppers, onion, quinoa, beans). Rich in antioxidants. Quinoa + beans drive the protein.
