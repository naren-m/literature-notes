# Mahishasura Mardini Stotram — "Aigiri Nandini"

Migration of the Notion page holding the famous **Mahishāsura-Mardini Stotram** — the 21-verse devotional hymn to Goddess Durga that is traditionally chanted during **Navarātri** and at the dawn of **Mahālaya** (the beginning of Durga Pujā). The hymn is usually called by its opening words **"Ayi Giri Nandini"** ("O Daughter of the Mountain King"). The Notion page contained the full Sanskrit text along with a line-by-line English meaning commentary sourced from vak1969.com — preserved below.

## Context and authorship

"**Mahishāsura-Mardhini**" means "She Who Killed the Asura Mahishasura" and refers to Goddess **Durga**. The demon Mahishasura took the form of a buffalo (Sanskrit *mahiṣa* = buffalo), which is why he is called "Mahisha Asura". Durga was manifested by the combined tejas (fiery energy) of the Gods specifically to eradicate evil, and she slew Mahishasura and many other asuras and dānavas.

The hymn's authorship is disputed:
- Some sources attribute it to **Kavi Rāmakriṣṇa** (i.e. **Tenāli Rāma** of the Vijayanagara Empire, court poet to Krishnadeva Raya).
- Others attribute it to **Ādi Śaṅkara** (8th c. CE).

Though called "Mahishāsura Mardhini", the verses actually describe the Devi in three aspects: **Durgā, Lakṣmī, and Sarasvatī** — so the hymn is implicitly a Tridevi invocation.

> *"The place where Sri Mahishāsura Mardini Stotram is sung every day, I will always be present and never leave."*
> — The Devi's proclamation in the 12th chapter of the **Devi Mahātmyam**

The hymn removes fear, sadness, doubt, anger, ego, and inertia, and is said to remove obstacles from the devotee's path. Listening confers benefit, but **recitation is considered more powerful**.

## Verse 1 — "Ayi Giri Nandini"

**Sanskrit/English transliteration:**

> *Ayi Giri Nandini Nanditha Medini Vishwa Vinodhini Nanda Nuthe*
> *Girivara Vindhya Shirodhini Vassini Vishnu Vilassini Jishnu Nuthe*
> *Bhagawathi Hey Shithi Kantha Kutumbini Bhuri Kutumbini Bhuri Krithe*
> *Jaya Jaya Hey Mahishasura Mardhini Ramya Kapardhini Shaila Suthe*

**Meaning:**
- **Ayi** — Divine Supreme Mother
- **Giri Nandini** — Daughter of the king of the mountains
- **Nanditha Medini** — (1) Worthy of prayer; (2) Sita, daughter of Mother Earth
- **Vishwa Vinodini** — Universal power that brings life to the Universe
- **Nanda Nuthe** — Praised by Nanda, foster-father of Lord Krishna
- **Girivara Vindhya Shirodhini Vassini** — Resident of the peak of the Vindhya mountain range
- **Vishnu Vilassini** — Worshipped by Lord Vishnu
- **Jishnu Nuthe** — Worshipped and adored by Lord Indra
- **Bhagavathi** — Mahalakshmi
- **Shithi Kantha Kutumbini** — Wife of Lord Shiva (the "blue-throated")
- **Bhuri Kutumbini** — Mother of the whole universe
- **Bhuri Krithe** — Fulfiller of all wishes, guardian of the universe
- **Jaya Jaya** — Victory, victory
- **Mahishasura Mardhini** — Slayer of the demon Mahishasura
- **Ramya Kapardhini** — Of beautiful hair (a tribute to the Goddess's charm)
- **Shaila Suthe** — Daughter of Shailendra, Lord of the mountains

## Verse 2 — "Suravara Varshini"

> *Suravara Varshini Durdhara Dharshini Durmukha Marshini Harsharathe*
> *Tribhuvana Poshini Shankara Toshini Kilbisha Moshini Ghosharathe*
> *Dhanujani Roshini Dhithisutha Roshini Durmadha Shoshini Sindhusuthe*
> *Jaya Jaya Hey Mahishasura Mardhini Ramya Kapardhini Shaila Suthe*

Describes her as bestower of boons to the Devas, slayer of the demons Durdhara and Durmukha, giver of food to the three worlds, one who pleases Shiva and destroys sin, one who bears anger against the Asuras, daughter of the king of the oceans (Mahalakshmi).

## Verse 3 — "Ayi Jagadamba"

> *Ayi Jagadamba Madhamba Kadamba Vanapriya Vaasini Haasarathe*
> *Shikhari Shiromani Tungaahimalaya Shringanijaalaya Madhyagathe*
> *Madhu Madhure Madhu Kaithabha Ghanjini Kaithabha Bhanjini Raasarathe*

Universal Mother, fond of the evergreen Kadamba forests, dwelling in the high peaks of the Himalayas, vanquisher of demons Madhu and Kaiṭabha, provider of divine enjoyment.

## Verse 4 — "Shatha Khanda Vikhandita"

> *Ayi Shatha Khanda Vikhandita Runda Vithunditha Shunda Gajaadipathe*
> *Ripugaja Ganda Vidharuna Chanda Parakraama Shunda Mrigaadhi Pathe*
> *Nija Bhuja Dhanda Nipathith Khanda Vipathitha Munda Bhataadipathe*

She cuts the enemy into a hundred pieces, destroys the elephants of Asura Sunda's army, and with her own hands delivers punishment to Chanda and Munda.

## Verse 5 — "Rana Dhurmadha"

> *Ayi Rana Dhurmadha Shathru Vadhoodhitha Dhurdhara Nirjara Shakti Bruthe*
> *Chatura Vichaara Dhureena Maha Shiva Dhoothakritha Pramadhipathe*
> *Dhuritha Dhureeha Dhuraashaya Dhurmathi Danava Dhootha Kruthaantha Mathe*

Defeats those with evil intentions, vanquishes evil forces, uses four intelligent strategies, enlists Shiva as an emissary to the Dhanavas — giving even the wicked Asuras a chance to reform.

## Verse 6 — "Sharanagatha Vairi Vadhu Vara"

> *Ayi Sharanagatha Vairi Vadhuvara Veera Varabhaya Dhayakare*
> *Tribhuvana Masthaka Shoola Virodhi Shirodhi Krithamala Shoolakare*
> *Dhumi Dhumi Thamaara Dhundhubi Nada Maho Mukha Reetkritha Thigmakare*

Compassionate to the wives of enemies who surrender at her feet, she impales vanquished asuras on her spear and makes a garland of their heads; the sound of her trumpet announces war.

## Verse 7 — "Nija Hoomkrithi"

> *Ayi Nija Hoomkrithi Maathra Nirakritha Dhoomra Vilochana Dhoomra Shathe*
> *Samara Vishoshitha Shonita Beeja Samudhbhava Shonitha Beeja Lathe*
> *Shiva Shiva Shumbha Nishumbha Mahaahava Tharpitha Bhutha Pishaacharathe*

By chanting the Bīja mantra "Hum", she destroys the red-eyed Asura Dhūmra-Vilocana. She drinks up the blood of Rakta-Bīja (every drop of whose blood would create more copies of him), and gives liberation to Shumbha and Nishumbha by letting them die chanting Shiva's name.

## Verse 8 — "Dhanu Ranu Sanga"

> *Dhanu Ranu Sanga Ranakshana Sanga Parishpura Dhanga Natak Katake*
> *Kanaka Pishanga Prishatka Nishanga Rasadh Bhata Shringa Hathaa Vatuke*
> *Kritha Chaturanga Balakshiti Ranga Ghatad Bahuranga Ratad Batuke*

Devi looks beautiful holding her bow; she addresses four armies of the enemy single-handedly and demolishes the wicked Asuras in every hue — she is almighty.

## Verse 9 — "Jaya Jaya Japya Jaye"

> *Jaya Jaya Japya Jaye Jaya Shabdha Parasthuthi Tathpara Vishwanuthe*
> *Bhana Bhana Bhinjjini Bhinkritha Noopura Sinjitha Mohitha Bhutapathe*
> *Natitha Nataartha Natee Nata Nayaka Naatitha Naatya Sugaanarathe*

The world is dazzled by her divine dance. Her anklets make beautiful musical sounds; Lord Shiva himself is mesmerised watching her; she dances the cosmic dance like Nataraja.

## Verse 10 — "Sumanah Sumanah"

> *Ayi Sumanah Sumanah Sumanah Sumanah Sumanohara Kaanthiyuthe*
> *Shritha Rajanee Rajanee Rajanee Rajanee Rajanee Kara Vakra Vrithe*
> *Sunayana Vibhrama Rabhrama Rabhrama Rabhrama Rabhrama Radhipathe*

Of good heart, lovely form, shining like a full moon on a dark night; queen of the night, with a face perfect like a full moon; her eyes are always on the lookout protecting her devotees.

## Verse 11 — "Sahitha Maha Hava"

> *Sahitha Maha Hava Mallama Thallika Mallitha Rallaka Mallarathe*
> *Virachitha Vallika Pallika Mallika Bhillika Bhillika Varga Vrithe*
> *Sithakritha Phulla Samulla Sithaaruna Thallaja Pallava Sallalithe*

Powerful enough to combat massive Asura wrestlers; dwells in Himalayan forests amid pepper and jasmine creepers, in a setting of lilies and fresh grass.

## Verse 12 — "Avirala Ganda"

> *Avirala Ganda Galanmadha Medhura Maththa Mathangaja Raaja Pathe*
> *Tribhuvana Bhushana Bhootha Kalaanidhi Rupa Payonidhi Raaja Suthe*
> *Ayi Sudha Theejana Laalasa Maanasa Mohana Manmatha Raaja Suthe*

Makes her army of elephants intoxicated and uncontrollable in battle. Adorned with jewels; embodiment of all arts; daughter of the Himalayas; casts a spell on minds with beauty and intellect.

## Verse 13 — "Kamala Dhalamala"

> *Kamala Dhalamala Komala Kaanthi Kallakali Thamala Bhaalalathe*
> *Sakala Vilaasa Kalaanila Yakrama Keli Chalathkala Hamsakule*
> *Alikula Sankula Kuwalaya Mandala Mouli Milad Bhakulaali Kule*

Face adorned by the crescent moon; expert in all art forms; resides in thick forests surrounded by Lily, Jasmine, and Bakula flowers, with bees humming around.

## Verse 14 — "Khara Murali Rava"

> *Khara Murali Rava Veejitha Koojitha Lajjitha Kokila Manjumathe*
> *Militha Pulindha Manohara Gunjitha Ranjitha Shaila Nikunjagathe*
> *Nijaguna Bhutha Maha Sabari Gana Sadhguna Sambhrita Kelithale*

Plays divine flute music sweeter than the shy Cuckoo's song; mingles with the tribal women and local Sabari clan; a person of pure thoughts and actions.

## Verse 15 — "Katitata Peetha"

> *Katithata Peetha Dhukoola Vichitra Mayukha Thiraskritha Chandra Ruche*
> *Pranatha Surasura Mauli Mani Sphura Dhanshula Sannakha Chandra Ruche*
> *Jita Kanaakachala Mauli Padhorjitha Nirbhara Kunjara Kumbhakuche*

Wears a yellow drape on slim hips; worshipped by all including Asuras; shines brighter than golden Mount Meru.

## Verse 16 — "Vijeetha Sahasra"

> *Vijeetha Sahasra Karaika Sahasra Karaika Sahasra Karaikanuthe*
> *Kritha Sura Tharaka Sangara Tharaka Sangara Tharaka Sonu Suthe*
> *Suratha Samaadhi Samaana Samaadhi Samaadhi Samaadhi Sujatharathe*

Eternally victorious; gave weapons to Subrahmaṇya (her son) to kill the demon Tāraka; blessed King Suratha and the merchant Samādhi (from the Devī Māhātmya frame story) with her Bīja mantra for liberation.

## Verse 17 — "Pada Kamalam"

> *Pada Kamalam Karuna Nilaye Varivasya Dhiyonudhi Namsha Shive*
> *Ayi Kamale Kamalaa Nilaye Kamala Nilaye Sa Kathamna Bhavet*
> *Thava Padhameva Param Padha Mithyanu Sheelayatho Mama Kimna Shive*

Tender lotus feet; ocean of compassion; showers abundance on those who meditate on her. She can transform a beggar to a multi-millionaire.

## Verse 18 — "Kanakala Sathkala"

> *Kanakala Sathkala Sindhu Jalairanu Sinjjinuthe Guna Ranga Bhuwam*
> *Bhajathi Sakimna Sachee Kucha Kumbha Thatipari Rambha Sukha Anubhavam*
> *Thava Charanam Sharanam Karavaani Nataamara Vaani Nivasi Shivam*

Here she is praised as Sarasvatī; all you need is to dip flowers in water and offer abhiṣeka to her; she is the source of all knowledge (Vedas) and music.

## Verse 19 — "Thava Vimalendhu Kulam"

> *Thava Vimalendhu Kulam Vadhanaendhu Malam Sakalam nanu Koolayathe*
> *Kimu Purohitha Purendhu Mukhi Sumukhi Bhirasow Vimukhee Kriyathe*
> *Mamathu Matham Shiva Naamadhane Bhavathi Kripaya Kimutha Kriyathe*

Face is pure and shining like the moon; belongs to the family of the Moon; smiling face encourages devotees.

## Verse 20 — "Ayi Mayi Deena Dayalu" (closing)

> *Ayi Mayi Deena Dayalu Daya Kripayaiva Tvayaa Bhavi Thavya Mume*
> *Ayi Jagatho Janani Kripayasi Yathaasi Thathanu Mithasi Rathe*
> *Yaduchita Mathra Bhavathyurare Krututhadhuru Thapa Mapaakurutha*

The closing prayer: "O Mother, please show mercy on us poor weak souls; please manifest and shower compassion whenever we need your help; during times of stress a mere look at your face is cooling to the mind."

## Learning to chant

The Notion page included the author's own advice: it is a **4–5 hour effort** to learn the basic chanting. Download an accompanying audio/YouTube rendition onto an app that allows pace adjustment, print a copy of the lyrics, and practice stanza by stanza matching the metre and rhythm. Understand the meaning while chanting — this is what turns recitation into devotion.
